# Certificates deep dive

## [](#_environment)Environment

Choose your **Enviroment** in the dropdown. This is typically `prod` or `dev`.

## [](#_filters)Filters

There are multiple filters that you can apply when viewing your certificates.

These include:

-   **Type**: The type of the certificate (such as *Managed* or *Custom*).

-   **Method**: The method of validation (such as `HTTP01` or `DNS01`).

-   **Status**: The status of the certificate.

    -   **Valid**: the certificate is validated and active.

    -   **In progress**: the certificate has been added and is being validated.

    -   **Not signed**: the certificate is uploaded but not [signed](https://docs.magnolia-cms.com/paas/cockpit/sections/networking/#_sign_certificate) (not validated).

    -   **Error/Expired**: the certificate is not valid due to being expired or another error.



## [](#_add_a_certificate)Add a certificate

You can manage your own certificates directly in the Cockpit under the **Networking** section.

You can either choose to [">add a managed certificate](#Managed <span class=) or [">add a custom certificate](#Custom <span class=).

## [](#_name)Name

Name of the certificate

## [](#_status)Status

Specifies the status of the certificate.

-   **Valid**: the certificate is validated and active.

-   **In progress**: the certificate has been added and is being validated.

-   **Not signed**: the certificate is uploaded but not [signed](https://docs.magnolia-cms.com/paas/cockpit/sections/networking/#_sign_certificate) (not validated).

-   **Error/Expired**: the certificate is not valid due to being expired or another error.


## [](#_valid_from)Valid from

The date from which the certificate is valid.

-   **Format**: Month, DD, YYYY, HH:MM AM/PM

-   **Example**: March 17, 2026, 4:20 PM


## [](#_valid_to)Valid to

The date to which the certificate is valid.

-   **Format**: Month, DD, YYYY, HH:MM AM/PM

-   **Example**: March 17, 2026, 4:20 PM


## [](#_renews)Renews

The date when the certificate renews.

-   **Format**: Month, DD, YYYY, HH:MM AM/PM

-   **Example**: March 17, 2026, 4:20 PM


## [](#_alternative_names)Alternative names

The **Alternative name** for your certificate.

If a custom certificate, automatically fills the **Common Name** when created.

## [](#_issuer)Issuer

Specifies the issuer of your certificate.

-   If it’s a managed certificate, it’ll be with Lets Encrypt.

-   If it’s a custom certificate, it shows your certificate issuer.


**Example**: letsencrypt-prod via HTTP01

## [](#_custom)Custom

Specifies if the certificate is custom or not.

## [](#_created)Created

The date when the certificate was created.

-   **Format**: Month, DD, YYYY, HH:MM AM/PM

-   **Example**: March 17, 2026, 4:20 PM


## [](#_details)Details

Click the stacked lines ☰ to reveal more options such as:

-   **Delete**: Delete the certificate

-   **Reference**: Details the Kubernetes objects the current object relates to.

-   **Certificate details**: Review all details about the certificate.
